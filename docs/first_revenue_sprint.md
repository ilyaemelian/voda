# First Revenue Sprint

Date: 2026-05-25

## Goal

VODA should validate paid demand before overbuilding.

The first commercial milestone is simple:

**earn the first direct payments from families or students through low-friction digital products, previews, and subscriptions.**

## Revenue Thesis

VODA can start monetizing with lightweight self-serve offers while the full product is being developed.

The first offers should be:

- useful immediately;
- easy to deliver;
- low-support;
- connected to the larger VODA route;
- priced low enough for fast testing.

## Initial Offers

- admission checklist for final school-year students;
- olympiad season preparation checklist for grades 8-10;
- academic portfolio template;
- university application route mini-pack;
- Telegram-based monthly opportunity digest.

Example prices:

- 490 RUB;
- 990 RUB;
- 1,990 RUB;
- monthly test subscription from 500-1,990 RUB.

Primary channel:

- Telegram;
- direct payment links;
- parent communities;
- tutor partnerships.

## Validation Metrics

The sprint should measure:

- landing visits;
- product page views;
- checkout starts;
- completed payments;
- refund requests;
- email or Telegram opt-ins;
- upgrade interest;
- repeated use.

## Success Criteria

The first sprint is successful if VODA can generate:

- first 10 paid orders;
- first 50 leads;
- first repeat customer;
- first parent feedback;
- first signal that a paid subscription can follow a low-cost purchase.

## Strategic Role

Low-cost products are not the end goal.

They are the entry layer:

```text
template or checklist -> route preview -> subscription -> admissions support -> skills and career products
```

VODA should use small products to learn what families buy, build trust, and create a path into the larger subscription ecosystem.
